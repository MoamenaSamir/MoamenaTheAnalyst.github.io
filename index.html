Airplane Crashes in Power BI

This project uses Power BI to analyze airplane crashes spanning 1980 to 2023. Key objectives include temporal and geospatial analysis, evaluating operator and aircraft performance, identifying fatality trends, and analyzing crash patterns along routes. The dataset provides comprehensive crash details and fatality information.

Airplane companies seeking to enhance aviation safety are the primary audience. The methodology involves importing and cleaning the dataset to ensure data integrity. Visualizations created in Power BI facilitate interactive exploration of the data.

Key findings highlight Chicago O'Hare, Illinois, and specific routes as having significant airplane fatality rates. The year 2001 recorded the highest global airplane fatality rate. Relationships between aircraft registrations, types, operators (like American Airlines), and fatalities were identified.

Expected outcomes include actionable insights to improve aviation safety measures, enabling companies to implement targeted safety enhancements based on data-driven analysis.

Electronic sales in Power BI

This project uses Power BI to analyze electronics store sales from 2021 to 2022. Key objectives include improving low-selling products and increasing sales promotion for high-selling ones. The dataset offers detailed sales information for various electronic products, including robots, drones, robot kits, drone kits, ebooks, blueprints, and training videos.

The primary audience for this project is fans of electronic products. The methodology involves importing and cleaning the dataset to ensure accuracy and consistency. Visualizations created in Power BI facilitate interactive exploration of the data.

Key findings highlight that in 2021, robots had the highest sales rate and blueprints had the lowest, with June being the highest month of sales and December the lowest. In 2022, drones had the highest sales rate and blueprints had the lowest, with March being the highest month of sales and September the lowest.

Expected outcomes include actionable insights to enhance sales strategies, enabling the electronics store to improve low-selling products and boost promotions for high-selling ones based on data-driven analysis.

HR Analysis in Power BI

This project uses Power BI to analyze Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) within the HR dataset of a company from the perspective of workforce management and strategic planning. The dataset includes detailed information on employees, departments, branch locations, performance metrics, and salary details.

The primary audience for this analysis includes companies and Human Resources departments seeking to enhance their workforce management strategies.Methodologically, the project involves importing the dataset into Power BI, followed by comprehensive data transformation and cleaning to ensure data accuracy and consistency. Visualizations created in Power BI enable interactive exploration of the dataset, facilitating clear and actionable insights.

Key findings include: The male-to-female employee ratio favors males within the company. The average age of employees is approximately 37 years. The Research & Development (R&D) department exhibits the highest salary levels. A significant portion of employees hold bachelor's degrees. Additional information reveals that the company consists of three departments (Sales, HR, and R&D) operating across five branches.

Expected outcomes encompass providing valuable insights to optimize workforce management strategies and enhance strategic planning based on data-driven analysis.
